Decription

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish that's both hearty and comforting, perfect for feeding a crowd or enjoying as leftovers. Making lasagna involves layering pasta sheets with rich meat sauce, creamy béchamel or ricotta cheese, and plenty of melted mozzarella. The process starts by preparing a savory tomato-based meat sauce, usually with ground beef or pork, garlic, onions, and Italian herbs simmered together to build depth of flavor. Meanwhile, the cheese layer can be made from ricotta mixed with egg and seasoning, or by preparing a smooth béchamel for a more traditional twist.

Once the components are ready, assembly begins. In a baking dish, you layer sauce, pasta, cheese, and repeat until the dish is full, finishing with a generous topping of cheese to get that golden, bubbly crust. The lasagna is then baked until the layers meld together and the top is beautifully browned. After resting for a few minutes out of the oven, the lasagna slices cleanly and is ready to serve. It's a dish that takes a little effort but pays off in every cheesy, flavorful bite.
